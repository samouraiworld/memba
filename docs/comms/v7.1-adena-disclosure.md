Hi Adena team,

We just shipped a fix on the Memba backend (`memba.samourai.app`) for an internal-review finding we filed as `MEMBA-2026-001`. It's a Memba issue, not an Adena bug, but it touches the ADR-036 signing surface so I wanted to coordinate.

## TL;DR

* Memba constructed its ADR-036 sign document with `chain_id` hard-coded to `""`. That made the signature your wallet produced for our login flow chain-agnostic.
* In single-chain deployments (today, `test12` only) the practical risk is low. Once Memba runs on multiple Gno chains (we're prepping `gnoland1` / betanet next sprint), a token authenticated on chain A could have been replayed on a separate Memba instance on chain B.
* We've fixed it on the Memba side: signDoc now embeds the real `chain_id`; the auth `Token` carries the chain it was issued for; old tokens are grace-accepted for 24h then rejected.

## Why we're telling you

Because Memba's only exposure to wallets is via ADR-036, your team owns the other half of this protocol. We're not aware of any matching issue in Adena (and Adena has not been validated *as* having one — this is a heads-up, not a vulnerability report against Adena). But if you'd like, here are two things worth confirming on your end:

1. **Does Adena always pass a real `chain_id` when signing ADR-036 for arbitrary data?** If a dapp omits the field, what does Adena do — fail loudly, default to the wallet's current chain, or pass through empty?
2. **For session sub-accounts in v1.20+ (Multichain)**: same question for cross-chain session keys.

If you find anything in your codebase that mirrors what we found in ours, we're happy to coordinate timing of disclosure.
